Titan Classifications

“Not all monsters are made equal. Some crawl. Some walk. Some shape the sky.”

Clearance Level: SILVER and above

This section defines M.O.N.A.R.C.H.’s internal system for classifying Titans based on size, threat level, behavior, and containment viability. Designed to inform deployment, engagement protocols, and—when needed—termination orders.

TITAN CLASSIFICATION SYSTEM

All known Titans are categorized under the following size-based and behavioral classes. Secondary codes account for threat behavior and special anomalies.

01. CLASS: INFANT

  • Size Range: 1–3 meters
  • Threat Level: Low to Moderate
  • Containment Viability: High
  • Typical Behavior: Territorial, reactive. Can be solitary or part of brood systems.
  • Example Creatures: Needletooth Swarms, Mirelings, ect...

Recommended Response: Tranquilize and transport. Use netting, suppressants, and stun drones.

02. CLASS: JUVENILE

  • Size Range: 4–15 meters
  • Threat Level: Moderate to High
  • Containment Viability: Moderate
  • Typical Behavior: Often aggressive when provoked. Some show early intelligence or pack behavior.
  • Example Creatures: Cliff Howlers, Dune Shredders

Recommended Response: Small arms teams supported by armor/mech units. Capture advised only with heavy tranquilizers and suppression foam.

03. CLASS: ADULT

  • Size Range: 16–80 meters
  • Threat Level: High
  • Containment Viability: Low
  • Typical Behavior: Highly territorial, unpredictable. Capable of destroying vehicles and structures with ease.
  • Example Creatures: Hollow Maw, Ravager-class Serpents

Recommended Response: Full-scale division deployment. Orbital overwatch engaged. A.C.O.S. on standby.

04. CLASS: GIANT

  • Size Range: 81–199 meters
  • Threat Level: Critical
  • Containment Viability: Nearly Impossible
  • Typical Behavior: Exhibits intelligence and environmental influence. Often worshipped by local cults.
  • Example Creatures: Ironback Leviathan, Cloudpiercer

Recommended Response: Engage only with full mechanized battalions and aerial support. Civilian evacuation mandatory.

05. CLASS: TITAN

  • Size Range: 200+ meters
  • Threat Level: Omega (Extinction Event)
  • Containment Viability: NONE
  • Typical Behavior: Ancient, unknowable. Alters weather, terrain, or physics in localized areas.
  • Example Creatures: Obsidian Crown, The Sleeper Beneath, “Seraphim”

Recommended Response: A.C.O.S. Priority Strike Clearance.

“Observe only if you want to die knowing.”

06. CLASS: ALPHA

The “Alpha” classification refers to a kaiju that has established itself as the dominant apex predator within its region or globally. These titans are not only physically powerful but also exhibit strategic intelligence, territorial behaviour, and influence over lesser creatures.

In the M.O.N.A.R.C.H. Field Manual, Alpha-class kaiju:

Command instinctual obedience from lower-class monsters. Are considered priority threats due to their potential to destabilize ecosystems and trigger kaiju migrations. Often require full-scale deployment, including orbital strikes (A.C.O.S.), to neutralize. Are tracked continuously, even when dormant, under BLACK-4 to BLACK-5 clearance.

Only a handful of documented kaiju have ever reached Alpha status—Obsidian Crown being one of them.

BEHAVIORAL TAGS (SECONDARY CLASSIFIERS):

Used in combination with size class for tactical analysis.

  • DORMANT: Currently inactive or hibernating
  • ROGUE: Acts unpredictably, no clear purpose
  • GUARDIAN: Protects territory or specific biospheres
  • HIVE-LINKED: Responds to unseen alpha or queen entity
  • TELEPATHIC: Interferes with communications or cognitive function
  • BIOLOGICAL WEAPON: Engineered or altered through non-natural means
  • DIMENSIONAL ANOMALY: Violates known physical or biological laws
